STAYING SAFE IN HOTEL GOLDFISH
1 DO NOT SHARE YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION

Do not share your home address, phone number or school name with anyone you meet at Hotel Goldfish unless your parents or guardians give permission to do so.

Do not send your photo or name with anyone you meet at Hotel Goldfish.

Do not share your credit card, or bank details with anyone you meet at Hotel Goldfish.

Don't give your password to anyone, not even your best friend.

2 DO NOT MEET PEOPLE FROM GOLDFISH IN REAL LIFE

Do not meet up with anyone in real life without the consent of your parents or guardian. When meeting your Goldfish friend for the first time in person, make sure to do so in a public place, preferably with your parents or guardian present.
3 DO NOT TAKE PART IN WORRYING CONVERSATION

Don't feel obliged to take part in conversation if someone says something that worries you or makes you feel uncomfortable. Also, do not respond to unclean, suggestive, or intrusive questions or messages. Report the incident via the feedback page to the Hotel Goldfish staff.
4 DO NOT PLACE TOO MUCH TRUST IN OTHER USERS

Don't be too confident: a 12-year-old boy may actually be a 50-year-old man. Don't pretend to be someone other than yourself. For your security, you can use a nickname instead of your real name when checking in at Hotel Goldfish
5 DO NOT GET SCAMMED

Don't trust other users offers or promises that seem too good to be true. Theyre probably not.

Don't forget to use common sense in the Hotel Goldfish and elsewhere on the Internet. Be critical and take into consideration that someone may be trying to scam you.
